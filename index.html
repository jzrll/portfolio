Practicum Experience

A summary of my hands-on learning and contributions during my practicum placement.

Company Overview

PetroEnergy Resources Corporation (PERC) is a publicly listed Philippine energy company under the Yuchengco Group of Companies, established in 1994 to focus on upstream oil exploration. Over the years, it has expanded into renewable energy and power generation, aiming to provide reliable and sustainable energy solutions. Through its renewable energy arm, PetroGreen Energy Corporation (PGEC), PERC holds a diverse portfolio of geothermal, solar, and wind projects, both onshore and offshore, and maintains investments in multiple joint ventures across the country.

Nature of Task Given

My internship at PERC aimed to solve issues with scattered data and manual reporting by developing a centralized dashboard and database system. I was part of Team 1, which was assigned to the power generation department. My role focused on creating stored procedures in PostgreSQL to process data and developing API routes using FastAPI for data entry and retrieval. I used Postman to test the routes and ensure they worked correctly. This project allowed me to apply my backend development and data analytics skills while gaining some front-end experience and expanding my technical knowledge.

Total Hours Rendered

A total of 384 hours was rendered during the on-the-job training, covering project planning, development, testing, and documentation. These hours were spread over 10 weeks.

Conclusion

My on-the-job training at PetroEnergy Resources Corporation (PERC) was a valuable experience that enhanced both my technical and soft skills. I applied my knowledge in backend development, database management, and API creation using Python, FastAPI, and PostgreSQL, while also gaining exposure to frontend work with React. Collaborating with a large intern team and various departments taught me the importance of communication, teamwork, and structured data in building impactful solutions. This practicum not only improved my confidence and practical skills but also reaffirmed my passion for creating systems that drive efficiency and informed decision-making.
